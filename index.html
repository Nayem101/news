Donald Trump fires Wall St enforcer Preet Bharara, who refused to quit

Manhattan federal prosecutor Preet Bharara's refusal to step down after a call for his resignation set up a public battle with US President Donald Trump, who abruptly fired Wall Street's top enforcer on Saturday (Sunday NZT), months after asking him to remain in the post. Bharara's firing came a day after Attorney General Jeff Sessions asked for the resignations of 46 US attorneys who were appointed by former president Barack Obama. In November, Bharara said after meeting with Sessions and Trump that he was asked to stay on in the new administration and had agreed to do so. The move raises questions about whether or why the relationship changed, and will draw attention to the high-profile cases that Bharara's office is handling.
